Our Mission · Nuestra Misión

We walk to heal, we connect to grow.

Raíces Movement is a bilingual walking community rooted in wellness, connection, and reflection. We create a welcoming space where community members move their bodies, breathe fresh air, and share meaningful conversation — step by step, at their own pace.

Caminamos en armonía con la tierra, honrando la sabiduría ancestral y los ciclos de la naturaleza para crear un espacio de pertenencia.

Our Story · Nuestra Historia

Born from community care.

Founded by two friends from Mexican backgrounds who dedicated their lives to serving the community, Raíces Movement was born from a desire to weave traditional wisdom, mindful movement, and community care into everyday life.

Cada caminata nos invita a desacelerar, sentir nuestros cuerpos y conectar — con nosotros mismos, con la tierra y con la comunidad.

Reconocimiento de la Tierra · Land Acknowledgment

We gather on the ancestral lands of the Gabrielino-Tongva people. We honor their care for this land — past, present, and future. As we begin the Spring Equinox, may we walk with gratitude, awareness, and care.

Nos reunimos en las tierras ancestrales del pueblo Gabrielino-Tongva. Honramos su cuidado de esta tierra — pasado, presente y futuro. Caminemos con gratitud, conciencia y cuidado.

Choose Your Adventure · Elige Tu Camino

La Mirada Creek Park is a green space centered around La Mirada Creek. The 0.9-mile loop wraps the park's perimeter with two scenic bridges. Mostly gentle with some moderate sections, 32ft elevation gain, and 30–60 min estimated. Benches are available along the path for rest. Restrooms and water fountains available near the trailhead.

Gentle Loop · Camino Suave

Paved and mostly flat. Accessible for strollers, wheelchairs, and all mobility levels for most of the route. Note: a step down at mile 0.4 and stairs near mile 0.9 can be bypassed or turned around — it's a loop, so you'll always find your way back.

Full Adventure · Aventura Completa

Cross both bridges, take the hills, and explore the full loop including steps and varied terrain. Significant sun exposure on this route — bring water and wear a hat!

Seasonal Offerings · Talleres Estacionales

Shared wisdom, shared healing.

Four times a year — one each season — we gather after the walk for a community workshop. Aligned with the cycles of the earth, each gathering is led by a rotating facilitator from within the circle: a neighbor, healer, artist, or storyteller offering their gifts.

Offerings from the Circle

Herbal & ancestral wisdom · Somatic grounding · Creative expression · Emotional wellness · Mindfulness & atención plena · Storytelling & narración

Safety & Accessibility · Seguridad y Accesibilidad
🌎 Trail · Sendero

0.9-mile paved loop, mostly gentle with some moderate sections. At least 3ft wide. Benches along the path for rest. Restrooms and water fountains available near the trailhead.

💧 Accessibility · Accesibilidad

Mostly accessible for strollers, wheelchairs, and mobility equipment. Step down at mile 0.4 and stairs near mile 0.9 can be bypassed or turned around — the loop always brings you back to the start.

☀ Sun & Water · Sol y Agua

Significant sun exposure on the trail. Shade trees present but limited. Always bring water — fountains near the trailhead. Hats and sunscreen strongly recommended.

🌬 No One Left Behind · Nadie se Queda Atrás

We walk a closed loop with no traffic — everyone finds their own pace and naturally returns to the circle. Walk alone, in pairs, or with the group. You always come back. 🌿

🔥 Weather · Clima

Rain or Shine. Extreme heat (95°F+) or lightning: shortened shaded gathering or reschedule via community chat. Safety always comes first.

📍 Parking · Estacionamiento

Paved lot at 12021 Santa Gertrudes Ave. No van-accessible spaces with striped aisles. Street parking also available nearby.
