Our Mission · Nuestra Misión

We walk to heal, we connect to grow.

Raíces Movement is a bilingual walking community rooted in wellness, connection, and reflection. We create a welcoming space where community members move their bodies, breathe fresh air, and share meaningful conversation — step by step, at their own pace.

Caminamos en armonía con la tierra, honrando la sabiduría ancestral y los ciclos de la naturaleza para crear un espacio de pertenencia.

Our Story · Nuestra Historia

Born from community care.

Founded by two friends from Mexican backgrounds who dedicated their lives to serving the community, Raíces Movement was born from a desire to weave traditional wisdom, mindful movement, and community care into everyday life.

Cada caminata nos invita a desacelerar, sentir nuestros cuerpos y conectar — con nosotros mismos, con la tierra y con la comunidad.

Reconocimiento de la Tierra · Land Acknowledgment

We gather on the ancestral lands of the Gabrielino-Tongva people. We honor their care for this land — past, present, and future. As we begin the Spring Equinox, we remember that the earth has long been here before us. May we walk with gratitude, awareness, and care.

Nos reunimos en las tierras ancestrales del pueblo Gabrielino-Tongva. Honramos su cuidado de esta tierra — pasado, presente y futuro. Caminemos con gratitud, conciencia y cuidado.

Choose Your Adventure · Elige Tu Camino

La Mirada Creek Park is a unique green space centered around La Mirada Creek. The 0.9-mile loop wraps around the park's perimeter with two scenic bridges that cut through the center. Mostly gentle grade with some moderate sections, 32ft of elevation gain, and 30–60 minutes estimated. Picnic tables, benches, restrooms, and water fountains are available along the route.

Gentle Loop · Camino Suave

Paved and mostly flat. Stroller and wheelchair friendly for most of the route. Note: a large step down at mile 0.4 and stairs near mile 0.9 can be bypassed — our Sweep volunteer is always there to help.

Full Adventure · Aventura Completa

Take the bridges, tackle the hills, and explore the full loop with steps and varied terrain. Significant sun exposure — bring water and wear a hat!

Monthly Workshops · Talleres Mensuales

Shared wisdom, shared healing.

Once a month, following the walk, a community member leads a short 15–30 minute workshop. Workshops are led by rotating facilitators — neighbors, healers, therapists, artists, and storytellers.

Example Topics

Herbal & ancestral wisdom · Somatic grounding practices · Creative expression · Emotional wellness · Mindfulness & atención plena · Storytelling & narración

Organizing Committee · Comité Comunitario

Join our circle of leadership.

We are forming a small committee of 4–5 volunteers who share our values of wellness, cultura, and collective leadership. Help us sustain and grow the Raíces Movement circle.

  • Welcome new participants and support event logistics
  • Co-create monthly themes, plática, and workshops
  • Outreach & partnerships with local organizations
  • ~3–4 hours per month · biweekly walks + one planning meeting

Values: ancestral wisdom · shared leadership · radical inclusion.

Safety & Accessibility · Seguridad y Accesibilidad
Trail · Sendero

0.9-mile paved loop, mostly gentle (5% or less) with some moderate sections (5–8%). At least 3ft wide. Picnic tables, benches, restrooms, and water fountains along the route.

Accessibility · Accesibilidad

Mostly accessible for strollers, wheelchairs, and mobility equipment. Step down at mile 0.4 and stairs near mile 0.9 may need to be bypassed. Our Sweep volunteer is always there to help.

Sun & Water · Sol y Agua

Significant sun exposure on the trail. Shade trees present but limited. Always bring water — fountains available along the route. Hats and sunscreen strongly recommended.

Parking · Estacionamiento

Paved lot at 12021 Santa Gertrudes Ave. No van-accessible spaces with striped aisles. Street parking also available nearby.

No One Left Behind · Nadie se Queda Atrás

A Sweep volunteer always walks at the back of the group to ensure everyone returns safely to the closing circle.

Weather · Clima

Rain or Shine. Extreme heat (95°F+) or lightning: shortened shaded gathering or reschedule via community chat. Safety always comes first.
